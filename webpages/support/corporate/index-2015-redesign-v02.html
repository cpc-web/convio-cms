Giving to Central Park Conservancy

For 160 years, Central Park has provided New Yorkers with a scenic retreat from the stress of urban life, and has become an integral part of New York City’s culture, history, and economy.

Moreover, a thriving Central Park has proven to be a social and economic engine for New York City, responsible for more than $1 billion in annual economic activity and revenue for New York City.

Central Park Conservancy exists to restore, manage, and enhance Central Park for the enjoyment of all – and is committed to maintaining and improving Central Park in a beautiful and healthy state for generations to come, ensuring its continued impact on the City of New York. The Conservancy relies on private contributions, including from Corporate Partners, to raise 75% of the $57 million annual parkwide budget.

As the organization entrusted with the responsibility of caring for one of New York's legendary public spaces, our work is founded in the belief that citizen leadership and private philanthropy are the keys to ensuring that Central Park remains healthy, beautiful and thriving.

Volunteer with a "Day in the Dirt"

Foster teambuilding, community service, and environmental stewardship by spending a rewarding half-day volunteering in Central Park. The Day in the Dirt program is a unique opportunity for corporate groups to promote volunteerism while strengthening cohesiveness and camaraderie.

 Learn More

The opportunity to schedule a Day in the Dirt volunteer project is an exclusive benefit of the Corporate Partners Program. By joining the Corporate Partners program at the Pin Oak level, your team will have the unique chance to volunteer in one of New York’s most iconic landmarks.

Projects and group sizes are determined based on the Park’s needs, and may include weeding, painting benches, planting ground cover, or raking leaves. Your company will be acknowledged on signage displayed in the work area while activities are underway. If you would like to schedule a picnic after your volunteer experience, the Conservancy can provide vendor names and location suggestions.

Please note: Days in the Dirt can be scheduled on Tuesdays, Thursdays, and Fridays. Each session lasts from 10 am to 1 pm and is appropriate for 20-25 team members.

For specific questions about the Day in the Dirt program, please contact corporate@centralparknyc.org.
