The Central Park Conservancy’s Horticultural Sciences College Internship provides students with the opportunity to work side-by-side with Conservancy staff to build important life and career skills. Through hands-on work, students will learn more about the complexity of urban park management in a park that receives over 40 million visits a year and better understand the role they have in protecting living landscapes.

The internship is 10 weeks, June 6 – August 11.

Interns work Monday – Thursday, 7:00 am – 3:30 pm, 7:30 am – 3:30 pm, or 8:00 am – 4:30 pm (depending on position), at a rate of $14 an hour.

Housing is the responsibility of the intern. Interns will be provided with uniform shirts, hat, and rain- gear but must provide their own pants (no jeans) and boots.

Available Positions

Horticulture
Central Park is divided into 49 management zones, each cared for by a “zone gardener.” Each Horticultural Intern is assigned to a Zone Gardener to assist with the maintenance of landscapes in their zone. Duties include litter removal, irrigation, weeding, planting, pruning, mulching, and interacting with the public. Multiple positions available.

Woodland
Each intern is assigned to a Woodland Zone Gardener to assist them with the maintenance of woodland landscapes. Duties include litter removal, invasive plant removal, transplanting, cribbing/erosion control, rustic trail maintenance and construction, irrigation, pruning, planting and interacting with the public. Multiple positions available.

Landscape Architecture and Construction Management
Working with the staff of landscape architects at Central Park, this intern is involved in the oversight of the construction of various projects throughout the Park, the creation of as-built plans, the gathering of field data, and other related jobs. Single position available.

Data
The Central Park Conservancy uses Geographic Information Systems (GIS) to help manage Park features and work records. The Intern is assigned to the GIS Staff to assist with GIS projects. Duties would include assisting with field data collection, map creation, data analysis and use of ESRI ArcGIS. Single position available.

Conservatory Garden
The Intern will have the opportunity to assist garden staff and experienced volunteers with the horticultural maintenance of Central Park’s six-acre formal Conservatory Garden. Single position available.
