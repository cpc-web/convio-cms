Central Park Conservancy has informally supported other parks in a variety of ways for more than three decades. The Institute for Urban Parks helps other parks by providing direct assistance and advice, formal training, and on-site support.

Forum Seminar Series  

The Forum Seminar Series brings together Conservancy staff and other park professionals to share their experience and ideas about how to improve local parks.

Webinars  

The Institute for Urban Parks hosts webinars on a variety of topics for urban park managers from around the world. To learn more, sign up for Park Professional Updates.

Training  

In-class and field-based training programs offer New York City park professionals a formal opportunity to learn industry best practices and how the Conservancy applies them in Central Park.

Five Borough Program  

The Five Borough Program takes training and on-site support to other parks across all five boroughs in New York City. The program is currently focused on turf-related improvement projects in Macombs Dam Park/Heritage Field, Crotona Park, and Van Cortlandt Park in the Bronx; Fort Greene Park and Brower Park in Brooklyn; Bennett Park and East River Park in Manhattan; Tappen Park and Walker Park in Staten Island; and Astoria Park in Queens. Historic Harlem Parks Crew  

The Historic Harlem Parks Crew provides on-site support in Manhattan's Jackie Robinson, Marcus Garvey, Morningside, and St. Nicholas Parks. The Crew works with New York City Parks Department staff to create and implement a consistent planting plan, maintain lawns, and provide seasonal snow removal and leaf clean up.
